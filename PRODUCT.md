# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Bluesify is for general adult piano learners who are serious about jazz and blues practice. They may not be professional musicians, but they can read notation, understand chord symbols at a working level, and want a focused tool that helps them hear, see, and practice arrangement decisions one step at a time.

They are most likely using the app on an iPad at a piano or keyboard, with occasional web use on desktop for file management, review, or development workflows.

## Product Purpose

Bluesify turns a MusicXML lead sheet into progressive solo-piano jazz and blues arrangements, then explains the musical choices through score preview, MIDI playback, and teacher-style rationale. Success means the learner can move from a basic lead sheet to a playable arrangement while understanding what changed: voicings, guide tones, bass movement, tensions, and practice focus.

## Brand Personality

Focused, serious, tactile.

The product should feel like a practice instrument: direct, responsive, and close to the act of playing. It should support concentration rather than perform for the user.

## Anti-references

Avoid toy music app patterns: cartoon visuals, novelty piano keys, oversized playful controls, gamified badges, childish color, and beginner-app cheer.

Avoid busy DAW patterns: dense mixer controls, timeline clutter, waveform-heavy chrome, many simultaneous panels, tiny labels, nested inspectors, and visually noisy transport UI.

Avoid the current dark jazz-club trope as the main identity. Jazz atmosphere can appear subtly through music-specific structure and material choices, but the interface should not depend on lounge lighting, decorative grain, or theatrical stage metaphors.

## Design Principles

The score is the instrument surface. Keep notation central, legible, and physically comfortable on iPad.

Practice moves in steps. Levels, teacher rationale, playback, and annotations should read as one coherent practice loop rather than separate widgets.

Controls should feel playable. Touch targets, transport, tempo, and level selection should have the confidence and immediacy of hardware controls without imitating a DAW.

Reduce interpretation friction. The learner should understand what changed in the arrangement without scanning multiple competing panels.

Respect serious learners. The voice should be clear and musically literate without sounding academic, gamified, or patronizing.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast for text and controls. The iPad layout should support touch comfortably at the piano, including large controls, clear focus states, and readable score/teacher text at common tablet distances.

Motion should be minimal, purposeful, and disabled or simplified under reduced-motion preferences. Color should not be the only way to communicate level state, warnings, or playback state.
